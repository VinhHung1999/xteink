## Voice Principles

### 1. Speak to One Person
Never write for "users" or "customers." Write as if you are talking to one person sitting across a cafÃ© table. Use "you" (not "our users" or "customers"). Use "we" sparingly â€” only when sharing the brand's genuine beliefs.

### 2. Invite, Don't Command
The brand never demands. It opens a door. It offers a hand. It shows what is possible.

| Instead of (commanding) | Write (inviting) |
|------------------------|-------------------|
| "Buy now" | "Start reading" |
| "Sign up today" | "Join the quiet" |
| "Don't miss out" | "There's a spot for you" |
| "Subscribe to our newsletter" | "Get a letter from us, once a week" |
| "Order before it's gone" | "Yours when you're ready" |

### 3. Show the Feeling, Not the Feature
Every feature should be translated into an emotional outcome. The user does not care about 74 grams â€” they care about forgetting they're carrying it.

| Feature | Feeling Translation |
|---------|-------------------|
| 74g weight | "So light, you forget it's there" |
| 4.3" E-Ink screen | "Just words. Just you." |
| Physical buttons | "The satisfying click of choosing to turn a page" |
| 7-day battery | "Charge it Monday. Read all week." |
| MagSafe attachment | "Snap. Flip. Read." |
| No app store | "Nothing to do but read. Nothing to distract you." |
| SD card storage | "Your books. Your card. Yours forever." |
| $69 price | "All this, for the price of a nice dinner" |
| 20-book library | "Just enough to finish what you start" |

### 4. Be Honest About Flaws
The brand soul explicitly states: imperfection is part of the love story. Never hide limitations. Acknowledge them warmly.

| Instead of hiding | Write honestly |
|-------------------|----------------|
| (Don't mention no frontlight) | "No frontlight â€” you read in the light of the world around you" |
| (Don't mention no touchscreen) | "No touchscreen. Your fingers press buttons, not glass." |
| (Don't mention learning curve) | "It takes a couple days to make it truly yours. The community will help." |

### 5. Never Use Hustle Language
This brand is the antithesis of productivity culture. Never use:
- "Optimize" / "hack" / "crush it" / "level up"
- "10x your reading" / "read faster" / "be more productive"
- "Limited time offer" / "act now" / "don't miss out"
- "Unlock your potential" / "maximize" / "scale"

**Instead use:** "Take your time." "Begin when you're ready." "Read for the joy of it."

## Headline Formulas

For marketing pages and campaigns, headlines follow these patterns:

### Pattern 1: The Quiet Statement
Short. Declarative. Full stop. Let the silence after the period do the work.
- "Reading, redefined."
- "Your pocket-sized library."
- "Less screen. More story."

### Pattern 2: The Invitation
Second person. Present tense. An open door.
- "Start with one page."
- "Carry a thousand books in your pocket."
- "Read anywhere. Read anytime."

### Pattern 3: The Paradox
Juxtaposition that mirrors the product's contradictions.
- "Small in size. Infinite in possibilities."
- "74 grams. A thousand worlds."
- "The quietest rebellion you'll ever join."

### Pattern 4: The Feeling
Describe what it feels like, not what it does.
- "The click of the page button. The silence of choosing a book over a feed."
- "That feeling when you've read more this week than all of last year."
- "A cafÃ©, a book, nowhere to be."

## Words We Love / Words We Avoid

### Words We Love
- Quiet, still, slow, gentle, warm
- Read, story, page, chapter, book
- Discover, become, begin, choose, find
- Pocket, carry, always, yours, own
- Simple, small, light, permission, freedom

### Words We Avoid
- Optimize, hack, crush, hustle, grind
- Revolutionary, disrupting, game-changing
- Limited, exclusive, premium (unless describing materials)
- Smart, intelligent, AI-powered (we are anti-tech-hype)
- Click here, submit, proceed (too transactional)
- Users, customers, consumers (say "you" or "readers")
